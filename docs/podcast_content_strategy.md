## 1. Content Direction

The first podcast produced by the application will be an introductory episode for the book *The Quality of the Question*.

The episode will not be:

- A complete audiobook
- A word-for-word reading
- A full summary of every chapter
- Only a summary of Chapter 1

Instead, it will be:

> An introductory book podcast that explains the central premise of *The Quality of the Question* and uses a selected Chapter 1 story as a narrative hook.

The purpose is to introduce the listener to the book, communicate its main value, and create enough curiosity for the listener to continue exploring it.

---

## 2. Proposed Episode

### Working Title

**The Quality of the Question: Why Better Questions Build Better Businesses**

### Alternative Titles

- **What Is This Problem Really About?**
- **Before the Idea, Ask the Question**
- **The Question Behind Every Business**
- **Better Questions, Better Businesses**

The final title may be selected after testing the generated script and audio.

---

## 3. Episode Objective

The episode should help the listener understand:

- What the book is about
- Who the book is for
- What makes it different from a traditional entrepreneurship book
- What journey the book offers
- Why entrepreneurship begins with a question
- How Chapter 1 introduces the central premise
- Why better questions lead to better decisions

The episode should leave the listener with one honest reflection question and one practical action.

---

## 4. Intended Audience

The primary audience includes:

- Students interested in entrepreneurship
- Early-stage entrepreneurs
- First-time founders
- People developing a business idea
- Professionals working in innovation
- People who feel stuck between having an idea and knowing what to do next

The episode should be understandable without requiring previous knowledge of entrepreneurship, Design Thinking, finance, or systems thinking.

## 6. Core Message

The central message of the episode is:

> Entrepreneurship does not begin with the perfect idea. It begins with the willingness to ask what the problem is really about.

The episode should explain that the book is not a collection of instant answers or motivational formulas.

Instead, it helps readers ask better questions about:

- The problem they are solving
- The idea they are developing
- The market they are entering
- The customer they are serving
- The system they are building
- The context in which the business must operate
- The financial reality behind the idea
- The next decision they need to make

---

## 7. What Makes the Book Different

The podcast should communicate that *The Quality of the Question* does not promise certainty.

It teaches the reader how to move forward while uncertainty is still present.

The book invites readers to:

- Question assumptions
- Observe before proposing solutions
- Separate ideas from real insights
- Read market signals honestly
- Identify a realistic first customer
- Understand the business as a connected system
- Recognize contextual risks
- Read financial information
- Test whether the numbers truly work
- Decide what to do next

The podcast should sound practical and honest rather than motivational or promotional.

---

## 8. Book Journey

The episode should briefly present the progression of the book.

### Chapter 1 — The First Question

The journey begins by asking what the problem is really about.

### Chapter 2 — The Idea Is Not the Business

The reader learns to distinguish an idea from an insight grounded in observation.

### Chapter 3 — The Market Does Not Lie, But It Does Not Tell You Everything

The book explores how to read market signals without using research only to confirm existing beliefs.

### Chapter 4 — Your First Customer Is Not Everybody

The reader defines a specific first customer and a realistic entry market.

### Chapter 5 — Your Business Is a System, Not a To-Do List

The book introduces relationships, feedback loops, bottlenecks, and second-order effects.

### Chapter 6 — Context Changes Everything

The reader examines whether the solution can work in the actual environment where it must operate.

### Chapter 7 — Money Is Not the Enemy, It Is the Language

The book explains why entrepreneurs must understand the financial language of their own business.

### Chapter 8 — Do the Numbers on Your Idea Actually Add Up?

The reader tests costs, margins, volume, and break-even assumptions.

### Chapter 9 — Now What?

The final chapter helps the reader locate where they are stuck and identify the next meaningful move.

The podcast should not explain every chapter in detail.

It should present this journey as a coherent progression.

---

## 9. Chapter 1 Narrative Hook

The main story used in the episode will come from Chapter 1.

Paola receives a Design Thinking assignment to create a better library space for students.

Her first reaction is to begin designing solutions:

- Rearranging furniture
- Adding outlets
- Thinking about the physical space

Before the students can propose anything, the professor asks:

> What is this problem really about?

During the exercise, the apparent problem changes several times.

It first appears to be a space problem.

Then it appears to be a noise problem.

After speaking with students, the deeper issue becomes visible:

> The problem is one of legitimacy.

Students do not always feel they have permission to occupy the library unless they are studying in the expected way.

The physical environment is not the complete problem.

The experience is shaped by an unspoken belief about who the library is for and how the space is supposed to be used.

This story demonstrates the central lesson of the book:

> None of the original solutions would have solved the real problem.

---

## 10. Connection Between Chapter 1 and the Full Book

The Chapter 1 story should function as the entry point to the complete book.

The episode should connect the story to the wider journey through statements such as:

```text
Before building the solution, understand the problem.

Before trusting the idea, observe the market.

Before trying to reach everybody, identify the first real customer.

Before growing, understand the system.

Before transferring a solution, understand the context.

Before investing, make the numbers speak.
```

These ideas should be adapted naturally into narration and should not sound like an isolated checklist.

---

## 11. Episode Structure

The generated podcast script should follow this sequence.

### 1. Opening Hook

Begin with a question that immediately introduces the central idea.

Example:

> What if the quality of your business depended less on the answer you found and more on the question you were willing to ask?

### 2. Book Introduction

Introduce:

- The title
- The author
- The intended reader
- The central premise

### 3. What the Book Challenges

Explain that the book challenges the assumption that entrepreneurship begins with a brilliant idea.

### 4. Chapter Journey

Briefly explain the progression from problem definition to market, systems, context, finance, and action.

### 5. Chapter 1 Story

Tell the library story in a natural and concise spoken format.

### 6. Core Lesson

Connect the story to entrepreneurship:

- Founders often design solutions too early
- The visible problem may not be the real problem
- Evidence may require a change of direction
- Changing the question is not failure

### 7. Listener Reflection

End with one honest question.

Recommended question:

> What question are you currently avoiding because the answer might force you to change direction?

### 8. Practical Action

Invite the listener to write down the honest question behind their current idea or project.

### 9. Closing Teaser

Close with a statement that invites the listener into the book.

Example:

> This is where *The Quality of the Question* begins: not with the perfect business idea, but with the courage to ask what the problem is really about.

## 12. Source Text Strategy

The application will not process the complete manuscript.

A curated editorial source file will be prepared specifically for the mini-project.

Recommended location:

```text
data/source_texts/book_intro_source.md
```

The source file will contain four sections.

### Part 1 — Book Premise

A short editorial overview explaining:

- The book’s main argument
- The intended reader
- The transformation the book proposes

Recommended length:

```text
150–250 words
```

### Part 2 — Book Journey

A concise overview of the nine chapters.

Recommended length:

```text
150–250 words
```

### Part 3 — Chapter 1 Hook

A selected and authorized excerpt from the library story.

Recommended length:

```text
350–500 words
```

### Part 4 — Final Reflection

A short passage connecting the opening question to the ending of the book.

Recommended length:

```text
50–100 words
```

### Total Recommended Source Length

```text
700–1,000 words
```

This is long enough to demonstrate meaningful AI transformation while remaining short enough to control API cost and processing time.

---

## 13. Transformation Goal

The language model should not merely shorten the source.

It should transform it into a coherent spoken episode.

The transformation should include:

- Selecting the most relevant ideas
- Creating a natural introduction
- Connecting the book journey to the Chapter 1 story
- Removing repetition
- Adapting written language for spoken delivery
- Creating transitions between sections
- Producing a reflection question
- Producing a practical action
- Creating a final teaser

The final script should be shorter than the input source.

## 15. Editorial Rules

The AI must:

- Use only information from the provided source
- Preserve the author’s central meaning
- Preserve Paola’s perspective when describing personal experiences
- Clearly distinguish the author from the listener
- Adapt the text for natural speech
- Create transitions between ideas
- Keep the episode within the requested length
- End with a reflection question
- End with a practical action or invitation
- Present the book accurately

The AI must not:

- Invent personal experiences
- Invent quotations
- Invent statistics
- Add business cases not present in the source
- Claim that a proposed solution was implemented when the source does not say so
- Turn the episode into generic motivational content
- Copy the source word for word
- Reveal the complete content of the book
- Present the podcast as a replacement for reading the book
- Alter the author’s central argument

---

## 16. Human Review Requirements

The generated script must be reviewed before audio generation.

The editable review step should allow the team to verify:

- Factual accuracy
- Faithfulness to the source
- Natural spoken rhythm
- Correct chapter descriptions
- Appropriate tone
- Correct attribution
- No invented information
- No excessive repetition
- Suitable length
- A strong opening and closing

Paola will provide final editorial approval for the demonstration script.

## 17. Audio Direction

The first version should use:

- One narrator
- A clear and natural English voice
- A reflective pace
- No exaggerated promotional tone
- No background music for the MVP
- No complex sound effects

The audio should sound like an intelligent introductory podcast rather than:

- An advertisement
- A motivational speech
- A formal lecture
- A direct audiobook reading

---

## 18. Expected Listener Experience

At the end of the episode, the listener should understand:

1. The central premise of the book
2. Why questions matter in entrepreneurship
3. How the library story demonstrates the problem
4. What themes the rest of the book explores
5. Why they may want to continue reading or listening
6. What question they should ask about their own idea

The desired emotional response is:

```text
Recognition
    ↓
Curiosity
    ↓
Reflection
    ↓
Desire to continue
```

---

## 19. Success Criteria

The episode will be considered successful when:

- It introduces the complete book clearly
- It uses Chapter 1 as a compelling hook
- It does not reveal too much of the book
- It preserves the author’s meaning
- It sounds natural when spoken
- It is understandable without prior context
- It includes the journey across the nine chapters
- It includes one reflection question
- It includes one practical action
- It encourages the listener to continue exploring the book
- It remains between approximately 3 and 5 minutes
- It can be reviewed and edited before audio generation
